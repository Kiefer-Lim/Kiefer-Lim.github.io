Integration Tricks

King's Rule: $$\int_a^b f(x) dx = \int_a^b f(a+b-x) dx $$
Feynman's Trick: $$ I'(\alpha) = \int_a^b \frac{\partial}{\partial \alpha}(f(x,\alpha)) dx $$

0. Useful Integrals

(a) \( \int_0^\pi \frac{1}{a+b\cos(x)} dx = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{a^2-b^2 }} ; a > |b|\)
\( = \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{a+b(\frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2})} \cdot \frac{2}{1+t^2} dt \)
\( = \int_0^\infty \frac{2}{a(1+t^2) + b(1-t^2)} dt\)
\( = \int_0^\infty \frac{2}{(a-b)t^2 + (a+b)} dt \)
\( = \frac{2}{a-b} \sqrt{\frac{a-b}{a+b}} [ \arctan(x \sqrt{\frac{a-b}{a+b}}) ] _0^\infty\)
\( = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{a^2-b^2}}\)

(b) \( \int_0^\infty \frac{\cos(ax)}{x^2+b^2} dx = \frac{\pi}{2b}e^{-ab} \)
\( = \text{Re } \frac{1}{2} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{e^{iax}}{x^2 + b^2} dx ; \)
\( \frac{1}{2} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{e^{iaz}}{z^2 + b^2} \)
Push the contour upwards so it curves around in the +y direction; the contour's magnitudes disappear and only leave the residue value.
\( = \frac{1}{\cancel{2}} \cdot \cancel{2}\pi i \text{Res }[ \frac{e^{iaz}}{z+ib} ]_{z=ib} \)
\( = \pi \cancel{i} \frac{e^{-ab}}{2\cancel{i}b} \)
\( = \frac{\pi}{2b}e^{-ab} \)

(c) \( \int_0^\infty e^{-sx} \cos(bx) dx = \frac{b}{b^2+s^2} ; \int_0^\infty e^{-sx} \sin(bx) dx = \frac{s}{b^2+s^2} \)
\( = \int_0^\infty e^{-sx + ibx} \)
\( = \int_0^\infty e^{(-s + ib)x} \)
\( = [\frac{1}{-s+ib} e^{(-s+ib)x}]_0^\infty \)
\( \frac{1}{s-ib} \)
\( \frac{s+ib}{s^2+b^2} \)

(d) \( \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx = \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \cos(x) dx = \frac{\pi}{2}\ln(2) \)
\( 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx \)
\( = \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx + \int_{\pi/2}^{\pi} \ln \sin(\pi - x) dx \)
\( = \int_0^{\pi} \ln \sin(x) dx \)
\( = \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(2x) \cdot 2 \cdot dx \)
\( = 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln 2 \sin(x) \cos(x) dx \)
\( = 2 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} \ln(2) + 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx + 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \cos(x) dx\)
\( = \pi \ln(2) + 4 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx\)
Cancelling \( 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx \) from the top and bottom yields
\( 0 = \pi \ln(2) + 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx\)
\( \int_0^{\pi/2} \ln \sin(x) dx = -\frac{\pi}{2}\ln(2) \)

Feynman's Favourite Trick

(a) \( \int_0^\infty \frac{ e^{-yx} \sin(ax) }{x} dx = \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan(\frac{y}{a}) \)
\( I(y) := \int_0^\infty \frac{ e^{-yx} \sin(ax) }{x} dx \)
\( I'(y) = \int_0^\infty - e^{-yx} \sin(ax) dx = - \frac{a}{a^2+y^2} \)
\( \cancelto{0}{I(\infty)} - I(y) = [- arctan(\frac{y}{a})]_y^\infty = -\frac{\pi}{2} + \arctan(\frac{y}{a})
\( I(y) = \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan(y/a) \)

(b) \( \int_0^1 \frac{ x^a - 1 }{\ln (x) } dx = \ln(a+1) \)
\( = \int_0^1 [\frac{x^y}{\ln(x)}]_{y=0}^{y=a} dx \)
\( = \int_0^1 \int_0^a x^y dy dx \)
\( = \int_0^a \int_0^1 x^y dx dy \)
\( = \int_0^a [\frac{x^{y+1}}{y+1}]_0^1 dy \)
\( = \int_0^a \frac{1}{y+1} dy \)
\( = [\ln(y+1) ]_0^a = \ln(a+1) \)

(c) Frullani: $$\int_0^\infty \frac{f(ax) - f(bx)}{x} = (f(\infty)-f(0))\ln(\frac{a}{b}) dx $$ \(a,b>0\) and \(f\) is continuous on \( (0,\infty) \)
\( = \int_0^\infty [\frac{f(yx)}{x}]_{y=a}^{y=b} dx \)
\( = \int_0^\infty \int_a^b f'(yx) dy dx \)
\( = \int_0^\infty \int_a^b f'(yx) dy dx \)
\( = \int_a^b \int_0^\infty f'(yx) dx dy \)
\( = \int_a^b [\frac{f(xy)}{y}]_{x=0}^{x=\infty} dy \)
\( = \int_a^b \frac{f(\infty)-f(0)}{y} dy \)
\( = (f(\infty) - f(0)) \int_a^b \frac{1}{y} dy = (f(\infty)-f(0))\ln(\frac{a}{b}) \)
(d) Graduate Frullani: $$\int_0^\infty \frac{f(ax) - f(bx)}{x} = (\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{x} \int_{c>0}^x \frac{f(t)}{t} dt - \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} x \int_{x}^{d>0} \frac{f(t)}{t^2} dt ) \ln(\frac{a}{b})$$

1. Catalan's Constant

$$ G := \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{5^2} - \frac{1}{7^2} + \frac{1}{9^2} - \dots $$

(a) \( \int_0^1 \frac{\arctan(x)}{x} dx = G \)
\( \int_0^1 \frac{\arctan(x)}{x} dx = \)
\( \int_0^1 \frac{1}{x} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \cdot \frac{x^{2n+1}}{2n+1} dx = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \cdot \frac{1}{(2n+1)^2} = G \)

(b) \( \int_0^1 \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2+1} dx = \int_0^{\pi/4} \ln(\tan(x)) dx = -G \)
\( = [ \ln(x) \cdot \arctan(x) ]_0^1 - \int_0^1 \frac{\arctan(x)}{x} dx \)
\( = - G\)

\( = - \int_1^\infty \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2+1} dx \)

(c) \( \int_0^1 \frac{\ln(x+1)}{x^2+1} dx = \frac{\pi}{8} \ln(2) \)

2. Gamma Functions, Beta Functions

$$ \Gamma(x) := \int_0^\infty t^{x-1} e^{-t} dt \ (x>0) $$
(a) Click for Properties of Gamma Function
\( \Gamma(x+1) = x \Gamma(x) \)
\( \Gamma(n+1) = n! \) for \(n \in \mathcal{Z}^+ \)
\( \Gamma(1) = 0! = 1 \)
\( \Gamma(\frac{1}{2}) = \sqrt{\pi} \)
\( \Gamma(x)\Gamma(1-x) = \frac{\pi}{\sin(\pi x)} \)
\( \Gamma(x) = \frac{2^{x-1}}{\sqty{\pi}} \Gamma(\frac{x}{2}) \Gamma(\frac{x+1}{2}) \)
\( \Gamma(x+1) \approx x^x e^{-x} \sqrt{2\pix} \) (Stirling's Approximation)
\(\Gamma(x)\) is the unique function that satisfies 1. \( \Gamma(1) = 1 \); 2. \( \Gamma(x+1)=x\Gamma(x) \); 3. \( \ln \Gamma(x) \) is convex.

\( \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-\alpha x^2} dx = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{\alpha} \)
\( \int_{-\infty}^\infty x^m e^{-\alpha x^2} dx = \frac{1}{2\alpha^{(m+1)/2}} \Gamma(\frac{m+1}{2}) \)

$$ B(x,y) = \int_0^1 t^{x-1}(1-t)^{y-1} dt \ (x,y>0) $$
(b) Click for Properties of Beta Function
\( Beta(x,y) = \frac{\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)}{\Gamma(x+y)} \)
\( = 2 \int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^{2x-1}(\theta)\cos^{2x-1}(\theta) d\theta \) for \(n \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \)
\( = \int_0^\infty \frac{y^{x-1}}{(1+y)^{x+y}} dy \)
Useful Identities
(a) Product Rules of sines and cosines: $$\sin(x) = x \prod_{n=1}^\infty (1-\frac{x^2}{n^2 \pi^2}) $$
$$\cos(x) = \prod_{n=1}^\infty (1-\frac{x^2}{(n-1/2)^2 \pi^2}) $$
(b) Log-sine and Log-cosine Fourier Series: $$-\ln \sin(x) = \ln(2) + \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{\cos(2kx)}{k}$$
$$-\ln \cos(x) = \ln(2) + \sum_{k=1}^\infty (-1)^k \frac{\cos(2kx)}{k}$$
(c) Wallis' Product: $$\frac{\pi}{2} = \prod_{n=1}^\infty \frac{4n^2}{(4n^2-1)^2} = (\frac{2}{1}\cdot\frac{2}{3}) \cdot (\frac{4}{3}\cdot\frac{4}{5}) \cdot (\frac{6}{5}\cdot\frac{6}{7}) \cdot (\frac{8}{7}\cdot\frac{8}{9}) \dots $$
(d) Hyperbolic Tricks:

\( arcsinh(x/a) = \ln( x/a+\sqrt{(x/a)^2+1} ) \) ; \( \frac{d}{dx} \rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+a^2}} \)

\( arccosh(x/a) = \ln( x/a+\sqrt{(x/a)^2-1} ) \) ; \( \frac{d}{dx} \rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2-a^2}} \)

\( arctanh(x/a) = \frac{1}{2} \ln( \frac{a+x}{a-x} ) \) ; \( \frac{d}{dx} \rightarrow \frac{a}{x^2-a^2} \)

Other Cool Integrals!

(a) One with King's Rule and oddness: \( \int_0^\infty \frac{dx}{x^4 + 2x^2 \cos(2\alpha) + 1} = \frac{\pi}{4\cos(\alpha)} \)
\( \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{-dx/x^2}{1/x^4 + 2\cos(2\alpha)/x^2 + 1} = \int_0^\infty \frac{x^2}{x^4 + 2x^2 \cos(2\alpha) + 1} dx \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{1+x^2}{x^4 + x^2 (1 - 2 \sin^2(\alpha)) + 1 } \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{1 - 2x \sin(\alpha) + x^2}{ x^4 + 2x^2 + 1 - 4x^2 \sin(\alpha) } \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{\cancel{1 - 2x \sin(\alpha) + x^2}}{ \cancel{[x^2 - 2 x \sin(\alpha) + 1]} [x^2 + 2 x \sin(\alpha) + 1 ] } \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{dx}{ x^2 + 2x \sin(\alpha) + \sin^2(\alpha) + \cos^2(\alpha) } \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{dx}{ ( x + \sin(\alpha) )^2 + \cos^2(\alpha) } \)
\( = \frac{1}{4 \cos(\alpha)} \arctan(\frac{x}{\cos(\alpha)}) |_{\infty}^\infty \)
\( = \frac{\pi}{4 \cos(\alpha)} \)

(b) With recursion: \( \int_0^\pi \frac{\cos(n\theta) - \cos(n\alpha)}{\cos(\theta) - \cos(\alpha)} d\theta = \frac{\pi \sin(n\alpha)}{\alpha} \ (n\in \mathbb{Z}^+) \)
\( I_n(\alpha) := \) the above integral
Since \( e^{i(n+1)\theta} - 2 \cdot \cos(\alpha) e^{in\theta} + e^{i(n-1)\theta} = 2 e^{inx} (\cos(\theta) - \cos(\alpha)) \), we get
\( I_{n+1} - 2 \cos(\alpha) I_n + I_{n-1} = \text{Re} \int_0^\pi \frac{ e^{i(n+1)\theta} - 2 \cdot \cos(\alpha) e^{in\theta} + e^{i(n-1)\theta} + e^{i(n+1)\alpha} - 2 \cdot \cos(\alpha) e^{in\alpha} + e^{i(n-1)\alpha} }{\cos(\theta) - \cos(\alpha)} \)
\( = \int_0^\pi \frac{\cos(n\theta) \cancel{(\cos(\theta) -\cos(\alpha))}}{\cancel{\cos(\theta) - \cos(\alpha)}} d\theta = \int_0^\pi \cos(n\theta) d \theta = 0 \)
The roots of the equation for the above recurrence is \( I_n(\alpha) = C_1 e^{in\alpha} + C_2 e^{-in\alpha} = \frac{\pi \sin(n\alpha)}{\alpha}\)
